Product Designer + AI Strategist

Product Strategist 2020–Present
Product Strategist

Led discovery and strategic roadmap for an 11,000-employee global intranet at a Fortune 100 agriculture and fuel company. The content organization wasn't the problem — access and relevance were.

What Was Broken

An intranet with poor adoption across a globally distributed workforce.

What Was Hidden

Stakeholders believed content organization was the issue. The actual problem was that employees couldn't find content relevant to their role and location — a personalization and access problem, not a taxonomy problem.

What Was Possible

A roadmap reframing the intranet as a role-aware surface, not a document repository.

Senior Growth UX Designer 2022–2023
Senior Growth UX Designer

Built research operations from scratch, identified critical purchase blockers, and stepped into a de facto product ownership role to keep the vendor portal alive after leadership departure — with Fortune 1 retailer partnership at stake.

What Was Broken

No feedback loop between customers and product. 60–70% of behavioral data was skewed by internal employee testing, making every product decision unreliable.

What Was Hidden

The vendor portal had no internal champion after leadership left. Without someone holding the product vision, the 300–500 daily mission target with Walmart was at risk.

What Was Possible

Establishing research ops as infrastructure, not a one-time activity. Taking de facto ownership of the vendor portal — building the case, presenting to C-suite, reversing the deprioritization decision.

Product Strategist 2021–2022
Product Strategist

Hired to fix a failing CRM. 40+ interview hours and 1,200+ data points later, the finding was that the product wasn't the problem — the sales culture and data hygiene were. Recommended a pivot that no prior agency had surfaced.

What Was Broken

A 20-year-old CRM with collapsing adoption. Leadership believed the product needed new features.

What Was Hidden

Adoption wasn't failing because of missing functionality. It was failing because of data quality and a sales culture that had never been designed around the tool.

What Was Possible

Deprioritize the feature roadmap. Invest in culture and data integrity first. Lower cost, higher impact, faster path to adoption.

UX Strategy Consultant 2022
UX Strategy Consultant

Led product-level discovery for the Da Vinci robotic surgery training platform. Found that surgeons weren't disengaged — they were routing around the product. Delivered a two-tier roadmap separating quick wins from strategic investment.

What Was Broken

Surgeons not using the official training platform. Engagement metrics were low and declining.

What Was Hidden

Surgeons weren't disengaged — they had found workarounds, building informal communities on Facebook and YouTube. The product had been built without them.

What Was Possible

A two-tier roadmap. Quick wins to close the gap between the platform and the workarounds. Strategic investments to rebuild surgeon trust over time.

Product Specialist 2024–2026
Product Specialist

Designed adaptive progression and conversational intelligence systems for a health AI startup. Addressed user drop-off by scaffolding engagement from any starting awareness level — no psychological readiness required upfront.

What Was Broken

User drop-off at the front door. The product assumed users arrived with self-awareness and readiness to engage. Most didn't.

What Was Hidden

The barrier wasn't motivation — it was the product's implicit requirement that users already understand themselves before the tool could help them.

What Was Possible

A scaffolded progression model that meets users where they are and builds complexity gradually, removing readiness as a prerequisite.

About

Dan Benner

Product Owner · AI Strategist

I specialize in the moment before the roadmap — when the problem hasn't been correctly identified and the team is building toward the wrong target. My work starts with diagnosis: interviews, data, pattern recognition across domains. The deliverable isn't a wireframe. It's clarity.

Eight years across healthcare AI, drone logistics, enterprise CRM, agricultural technology, and veteran services. Every engagement started with a misdiagnosis I was hired to confirm — and ended with a different, better question.
